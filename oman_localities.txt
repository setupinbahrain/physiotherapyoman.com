Executive Summary
This register documents every administratively recognised locality in the Sultanate of Oman as of the 2020 General Census of Population, Housing and Establishments, conducted on 12 December 2020 by the National Centre for Statistics and Information (NCSI).
Oman's administrative structure consists of eleven governorates (Muhafazat / محافظات), each subdivided into a total of sixty-one wilayats (districts / ولايات). Below the wilayat tier, NCSI enumerates every locality with fifty or more residents — this dataset captures 2,143 such localities with their official 2020 census populations.
Oman's total recorded population in the 2020 census was 4,471,148 residents (with the full administrative total including establishments and institutional population recorded at approximately 4,617,927). The governorate breakdown is shown in the summary table below.

Methodology and Scope
Data Hierarchy
Country → 11 Governorates (Muhafazat) → 61 Wilayats (Districts) → 2,143 Localities.
Primary Source
All population figures are drawn from the Sultanate of Oman, National Centre for Statistics and Information (NCSI), General Census of Population, Housing and Establishments, enumerated on 12 December 2020. This is a full national census conducted every ten years; it is the authoritative source for Omani population statistics.
Aggregation
Because NCSI publishes wilayat-level and sub-locality data across multiple PDF reports rather than a consolidated machine-readable gazetteer, this register uses citypopulation.de (editor Thomas Brinkhoff, University of Applied Sciences Oldenburg) as the structured aggregator. Citypopulation.de is an independent reference used by the United Nations Statistics Division, the OECD, and academic demographers. Every figure in this document traces back to NCSI 2020 census publications.
Locality Threshold
Per NCSI publication practice and citypopulation.de editorial policy, every locality with fifty or more residents in the 2020 census is enumerated individually. Hamlets smaller than fifty residents are aggregated into their parent wilayat and are not published as individual localities in NCSI reports — they therefore cannot be listed in this register.
Arabic Names
Arabic names for the eleven governorates and sixty-one wilayats are drawn from official Omani government publications. Sub-locality Arabic transliterations are not available in machine-readable form at source. English transliterations of sub-locality names follow citypopulation.de's convention, which in turn follows NCSI's Romanised spelling.

Administrative Structure of Oman
The Sultanate of Oman is divided into eleven governorates (محافظات, muhafazat), following the administrative reorganisation codified in Royal Decree 114/2011 (which elevated the former Al Buraimi and Ad Dhahirah regions to full governorate status) and the subsequent 2020 boundary adjustments.
Each governorate is subdivided into wilayats (ولايات, districts), which are the primary administrative unit below the governorate level. Each wilayat is administered by a Wali appointed by the Sultan and reports to the Ministry of Interior.
Below the wilayat tier, localities are grouped into census enumeration areas. NCSI enumerates these at the following levels of disaggregation: wilayat centre (the administrative town for which the wilayat is named), neighbourhoods or hay (in urban wilayats), villages and sub-villages (qura, qaryah), hamlets, industrial zones, and institutional compounds (e.g. military bases, universities).

Limitations and Notes
Sub-50-Resident Hamlets
Localities with fewer than fifty residents in the 2020 census are not individually enumerated in NCSI publications. They are rolled up into their parent wilayat totals. These are typically remote Bedu camps, seasonal fishing stations, or individual farmsteads scattered across the desert interior (notably in Al Wusta, interior Dhofar, and the Empty Quarter border regions). This register cannot list them individually because NCSI does not publish their names.
Coordinates
Latitude/longitude coordinates for the approximately 2,143 sub-localities are not published in a structured form by NCSI and are not available in any single authoritative source. Major wilayat centres have coordinates available in GeoNames and OpenStreetMap, but extending this to the hamlet level for the entire country is beyond the scope of this register. For coordinate data, the reader is directed to OpenStreetMap's Nominatim API using the English locality names in this document.
Population Totals
The sum of enumerated sub-locality populations within a wilayat will generally be slightly less than the official wilayat total. This is expected — the difference represents sub-50-resident hamlets, institutional population not allocated to a named place, and adjustments for undercount. Governorate totals shown in the 'Summary by Governorate' table and in each chapter heading are the NCSI official figures, not sums of sub-localities.
Arabic Transliteration Variants
English spellings in this register follow NCSI's official Romanised form (e.g. 'Ṣuḥār' rather than 'Sohar', 'Masqaṭ' rather than 'Muscat'). Readers should be aware that many localities have multiple common English spellings — e.g. Salalah / Ṣalālah / Ṣalālah; Sohar / Ṣuḥār; Rustaq / Ar Rustāq / Ar-Rustaq — all referring to the same place.
Date of Compilation
This register was compiled in April 2026 from the most recent NCSI census (2020). The next Omani national census is scheduled for 2030.
